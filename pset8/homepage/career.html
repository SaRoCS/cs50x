Company 1

Position 1

In the jeep, he stepped on the gas. Even at thirty miles per hour, the serpent was nipping at the doctor’s heels. But soon, the temple’s power stopped and the serpent became once more, just a carving. “Well,” said Dr. Thompson, “ that’s one more thing I’ve outran,” and with that he drove off Eye in hand. Deep in the heart of the jungles of Mexico, Dr. Thompson was on the run. This time he was escaping the clutches of a panther he had angered while searching for food. For Dr. Thompson, being chased around by wild animals was a regular occurance. Throughout his life as an archaeologist, he had been pursued by everything from a herd of elephants to enraged natives. Most of these events occurred while he was searching for some historical artifact, but today was different. Today, Dr. Thompson was looking for something of myth, not historical fact. He was searching for the legendary Eye of Quetzalcoatl. In the old Aztec myths, this Eye could be found at the top of the temple to Quetzalcoatl. After studying maps and myths, Dr. Thompson had finally discovered the location of the temple.
